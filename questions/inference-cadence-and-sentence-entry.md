## The question

Inference splitting and sentence entry, for someone who pauses for thought while dictating.

I find it distracting when words appear **exactly** as I speak them (maybe just a neurotype thing), so I like the display to be **lagging — but not too lagging**, because then you have to wait. That's a point of UI friction worth documenting: if inference happens all in one pass, I have to wait for it to finish before I can (for example) hit enter to send the message in an AI conversation.

There's a middle ground. Deepgram, I think, gets this really right. Questions:

- What are the common actual intervals (partial cadence, endpointing, utterance-end)?
- What interval becomes a particularly useful point at which **sentence and paragraph boundaries** are easy to detect, even when someone pauses for thought while dictating?
